SUNDAY's FLAT BLOCK

Sunday's Flat Block is a meticulously designed one-story residential development spanning 381 square meters, with dimensions of 33.3 meters in length and 15.4 meters in width. The structure houses four well-appointed apartment units, each featuring four bedrooms with en-suite bathrooms, a lounge, a kitchen, and a dining area. The first-floor units are accessed via private stair halls and include a sit-out/balcony, fostering a seamless connection between indoor and outdoor living. The project aims to merge cultural heritage with refined aesthetics, drawing inspiration from traditional Igbo architectural principles while incorporating neutral, contemporary design elements. This fusion respects historical context while embracing modern functionality, ensuring that the structure resonates with both cultural continuity and contemporary living. The 1,011-square-meter site, characterized by a dynamic topography, has been strategically developed to optimize spatial use while adhering to building regulations and setbacks. The natural slope of the terrain enabled the inclusion of a basement, further enhancing space efficiency. Additionally, the design provides two parking spaces per apartment and a communal recreational area, enriching the overall living experience. The architecture of Sunday's Flat Block is not merely about form but an intentional response to its environmental and cultural context. The structure interacts with its surroundings, creating a harmonious balance between tradition, function, and modern innovation.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • drafting, and development,
  • Construction documentation, setting out, and project management,
  • Value engineering, material optimization, and perspective modeling.
