NELSON's HOUSE

Nelson's House is a thoughtfully designed single-story family residence that serves as both a primary dwelling and a seasonal retreat, nestled in a remote Igbo village. Spanning 252 square meters (18.5m by 17.1m), the structure is carefully planned to maximize space within a compact plot while respecting local cultural and communal land tenure constraints. The layout includes two bedrooms, a main lounge, a kitchen with a pantry, a dining area, and a winery on the ground floor. The first floor accommodates four additional bedrooms, a family lounge, an open void that enhances spatial connectivity, and a sit-out area that fosters a seamless indoor-outdoor experience. The design process was deeply influenced by the village’s communal setting, where land ownership and extended family expectations played a crucial role in shaping the final outcome. As construction progressed, design modifications were necessary to align with cultural preferences, demonstrating how architecture is not just a response to physical constraints but also a negotiation with social and familial dynamics. Beyond its functional layout, Nelson’s Home integrates traditional Igbo architectural elements with a modern approach, ensuring that the structure is both contextually relevant and adaptable to contemporary living. The project reflects an understanding of spatial hierarchy, cultural symbolism, and practical needs, creating a harmonious balance between heritage and modernity.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Conceptual and schematic design,
  • Design development and drafting (AutoCAD).
  • Construction documentation, setting out, and project supervision..
  • Value engineering and perspective modeling with Autodesk Revit.

Nelson’s Home is more than a residence; it is a case study in spatial adaptation, cultural sensitivity, and functional design, demonstrating how architecture can both navigate tradition and embrace evolving lifestyles.
